About the project

       Crop yield prediction is a crucial task in agriculture, as it can aid in decision-making and planning for farmers and policy-makers. In this project, we aim to predict crop yield using machine learning techniques. We developed two baseline models using Random Forest (RF) and Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). To better understand the importance of the input features in the models, we incorporated explainable AI techniques using SHAP to explain the feature importance. And then used model stacking to combine the predictions of the individual models. The R2 score was used as the evaluation metric.

       To improve the accuracy of the crop yield predictions, we then experimented with two deep learning models: the TabTransformer and the MultiModal Transformer with BERT for tabular data. The TabTransformer model utilizes a novel attention mechanism that enhances the feature selection process, allowing it to better capture the relationships between the input features and the target variable.

       We performed model stacking again using the TabTransformer and MultiModal Transformer models, and the results show that the proposed models outperform the baseline models, achieving competitive results on the crop yield prediction task. The results suggest that the proposed models could be useful for predicting crop yields in agriculture, which can aid in decision-making and planning for farmers and policy-makers.
